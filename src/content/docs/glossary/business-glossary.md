# Acme Corporation — Business Glossary

This glossary defines business and industry-specific terms used across Acme Corporation's six industry verticals. Terms are listed alphabetically and tagged with the primary subsidiary where the term is most frequently used. Many terms apply across multiple subsidiaries; the primary tag indicates where the term originates or is most heavily referenced.

For technology-specific terms shared across the organization, see the [Technology Glossary](technology-glossary.md).

---

## A

**ACORD** (Association for Cooperative Operations Research and Development). An industry standards body for the insurance and reinsurance sectors. ACORD defines standard data formats and messaging protocols used for policy administration, claims processing, and broker communications. Acme Insurance uses ACORD standards for data exchange with brokers and reinsurers.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**Actuarial Model**. A mathematical model used to assess and quantify insurance risk. Actuarial models analyze historical loss data, demographic factors, and market conditions to estimate the probability and cost of future claims. These models directly inform pricing, reserving, and underwriting decisions at Acme Insurance.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**AML** (Anti-Money Laundering). A set of laws, regulations, and procedures designed to prevent criminals from disguising illegally obtained funds as legitimate income. AML compliance requires financial institutions to implement customer identification programs, transaction monitoring, and suspicious activity reporting. Acme Financial Services maintains a dedicated AML compliance team and automated transaction screening systems.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

## B

**Basel III**. An international regulatory framework for banks, developed by the Basel Committee on Banking Supervision. Basel III establishes requirements for capital adequacy, leverage ratios, and liquidity coverage. Acme Financial Services maintains compliance with Basel III requirements across its banking operations, which influences lending limits, capital allocation, and risk reporting.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**Billing Mediation**. The process of collecting, validating, and transforming raw usage records (such as call detail records) into a format suitable for the billing system. In telecommunications, mediation sits between the network and the billing engine, ensuring that every usage event is accurately captured and rated. Acme Telco's mediation layer processes hundreds of millions of events daily.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**Broker** (Insurance). An intermediary who acts on behalf of a customer to obtain insurance coverage. Brokers evaluate the customer's risk profile, solicit quotes from insurers, and negotiate policy terms. Acme Insurance distributes a significant portion of its commercial lines business through broker channels, requiring standardized data exchange via ACORD protocols.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**BSS** (Business Support Systems). The collection of systems that a telecommunications operator uses to manage customer-facing business operations. BSS encompasses billing, customer relationship management, order management, and revenue management. At Acme Telco, the BSS stack is the primary interface between the business and its subscribers.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

## C

**Cart Abandonment**. The event in which a customer adds items to an online shopping cart but leaves the website or application before completing the purchase. Cart abandonment rate is a key eCommerce metric, and Acme Retail employs automated recovery campaigns — including email reminders and personalized offers — to convert abandoned carts into completed orders.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**CDN** (Content Delivery Network). A geographically distributed network of servers that deliver content to users based on their location, minimizing latency and improving load times. Acme Media uses multiple CDN providers to distribute video, images, and static content to audiences worldwide, with intelligent routing to optimize quality of experience.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

**CDR** (Call Detail Record). A data record generated by telecommunications network equipment that documents the details of a phone call or data session. CDRs include information such as the calling and called numbers, call duration, timestamp, and network elements involved. Acme Telco processes CDRs in near real-time for billing mediation and network analytics.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**Churn Rate**. The percentage of subscribers or customers who discontinue their service within a given time period. Churn is a critical metric in telecommunications and subscription-based businesses. Acme Telco monitors churn rate at the segment level and uses predictive models to identify at-risk subscribers for targeted retention campaigns.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**Claims Adjudication**. The process by which an insurance company evaluates a submitted claim to determine coverage, liability, and payment amount. Adjudication involves verifying the policy, assessing the claim against policy terms, and calculating the settlement. Acme Insurance's claims adjudication system handles both automated straight-through processing for simple claims and manual review workflows for complex cases.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**CMS** (Content Management System). A software platform for creating, managing, and publishing digital content. Acme Media operates a headless CMS that supports multiple content types — articles, video, podcasts, and interactive media — and distributes content to multiple front-end channels via APIs.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

**COBOL** (Common Business-Oriented Language). A programming language dating from 1959, still widely used in insurance, banking, and government systems. Acme Insurance's legacy policy administration system is written in COBOL and runs on mainframe infrastructure. The ongoing modernization initiative is incrementally migrating COBOL business logic to modern services.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**Content Workflow**. The end-to-end process by which digital content moves from creation through review, approval, and publication. At Acme Media, the content workflow includes editorial review, legal clearance, metadata tagging, transcoding (for video), and scheduling for publication across distribution channels.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

**Core Banking**. The central system that processes a bank's daily transactions, maintains account balances, and manages the general ledger. Core banking platforms are the operational backbone of retail and commercial banking. Acme Financial Services operates a core banking platform that processes millions of transactions daily and is undergoing phased modernization.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**Credit Scoring**. A statistical analysis performed by lenders to evaluate the creditworthiness of a potential borrower. Credit scores are derived from factors including payment history, outstanding debt, and credit history length. Acme Financial Services uses both external bureau scores and proprietary models in its lending decisions.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**Cross-Docking**. A logistics practice where incoming shipments are directly transferred to outbound vehicles with minimal or no warehousing. Cross-docking reduces storage costs and speeds delivery times. Acme Distribution operates cross-docking facilities at key hub locations to support time-sensitive fulfillment for Acme Retail and external clients.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

## D

**DRM** (Digital Rights Management). Technology used to control access to copyrighted digital content. DRM systems enforce licensing terms by encrypting content and requiring authenticated playback clients. Acme Media implements DRM across its video-on-demand platform to comply with content licensing agreements from studios and content owners.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

## E

**eCommerce Platform**. The integrated technology stack that enables online retail transactions, including product catalog management, search, shopping cart, checkout, payment processing, and order management. Acme Retail's eCommerce platform serves web and mobile customers and integrates with Acme Distribution for order fulfillment.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**Endorsement** (Insurance). A written amendment to an existing insurance policy that modifies its terms, coverage, or conditions. Endorsements may add coverage, exclude specific risks, or change policy limits. Acme Insurance processes endorsements through its policy administration system, triggering premium recalculations and notification workflows.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**ERP** (Enterprise Resource Planning). An integrated software system that manages core business processes including finance, procurement, inventory, and human resources. Acme Distribution uses an ERP system as the backbone for its warehouse operations, procurement, and financial reporting.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

## F

**Fault Management**. The process of detecting, isolating, and resolving faults in a telecommunications network. Fault management systems monitor network elements, correlate alarms, and trigger automated or manual remediation workflows. Acme Telco's fault management platform integrates with SNMP-based monitoring tools and the corporate observability stack.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**Fleet Management**. The administrative approach to managing a company's vehicle fleet, including acquisition, maintenance, routing, and compliance. Acme Distribution's fleet management system tracks vehicle location, fuel consumption, driver hours, and maintenance schedules across its delivery fleet.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

## H

**HLS/DASH** (HTTP Live Streaming / Dynamic Adaptive Streaming over HTTP). Adaptive bitrate streaming protocols used to deliver video content over the internet. Both protocols segment video into small chunks and allow the player to switch quality levels based on network conditions. Acme Media uses HLS and DASH to deliver video content across devices and network conditions.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

## I

**Impression**. A single instance of a piece of content (typically an advertisement) being displayed to a user. Impressions are a fundamental metric in digital advertising, and Acme Media's ad platform tracks impressions to calculate fill rates, revenue, and campaign performance for advertisers.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

**Inventory Allocation**. The process of assigning available inventory to fulfill customer orders across multiple channels (online, in-store, warehouse). Effective inventory allocation balances customer demand, shipping costs, and delivery speed. Acme Retail's allocation engine considers real-time inventory levels across all locations when routing orders.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**IoT Tracking** (Internet of Things Tracking). The use of connected sensors and devices to monitor the location, condition, and status of physical assets in transit or storage. Acme Distribution deploys IoT trackers on containers, pallets, and vehicles to provide real-time visibility into shipment location, temperature, and handling conditions.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

## K

**KYC** (Know Your Customer). Regulatory requirements that obligate financial institutions to verify the identity of their clients. KYC procedures include identity verification, risk assessment, and ongoing monitoring. Acme Financial Services implements KYC checks at account opening and periodically throughout the customer relationship to comply with AML regulations.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

## L

**Last-Mile Delivery**. The final leg of the delivery process, from the distribution hub to the customer's door. Last-mile delivery is typically the most expensive and logistically complex segment of the supply chain. Acme Distribution operates its own last-mile delivery fleet and also partners with third-party carriers for geographic coverage.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

**Loss Reserving**. The process by which an insurance company estimates the amount of money it will need to pay for future claims. Reserves are established for both reported claims (case reserves) and claims that have occurred but have not yet been reported (IBNR). Accurate loss reserving is critical to Acme Insurance's financial stability and regulatory compliance.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**Loyalty Program**. A structured marketing program that rewards customers for repeat purchases or engagement. Acme Retail's loyalty program offers points for purchases, personalized discounts, and early access to promotions. The program spans both online and in-store channels and provides valuable customer behavior data for analytics.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

## M

**MiFID II** (Markets in Financial Instruments Directive II). A European Union regulatory framework governing investment services and financial markets. MiFID II imposes requirements on transaction reporting, best execution, and investor protection. Acme Financial Services complies with MiFID II for its European wealth management and trading operations.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**MVNO** (Mobile Virtual Network Operator). A telecommunications company that provides wireless services to customers without owning the underlying network infrastructure. MVNOs lease network capacity from mobile network operators (MNOs) and operate their own customer-facing systems. Acme Telco originated as an MVNO and has since expanded its own infrastructure.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

## N

**Network Monitoring**. The continuous observation of a telecommunications network to detect performance degradation, faults, and security anomalies. Network monitoring systems collect metrics from network elements via protocols such as SNMP and stream telemetry. Acme Telco's network operations center relies on monitoring data to maintain service quality.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

## O

**Omnichannel**. A retail strategy that provides customers with a seamless experience across all channels — online, mobile, and physical stores. Omnichannel capabilities include unified inventory visibility, consistent pricing, and the ability to start a transaction on one channel and complete it on another. Acme Retail's omnichannel strategy is central to its competitive positioning.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**Order Fulfillment**. The complete process from receiving a customer order to delivering the product. Fulfillment includes order processing, inventory allocation, picking and packing, shipping, and delivery confirmation. Acme Retail's fulfillment pipeline integrates with Acme Distribution's warehouse and delivery systems.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**OSS** (Operations Support Systems). The collection of systems that a telecommunications operator uses to manage network-facing operations. OSS encompasses network management, fault management, performance monitoring, and service provisioning. At Acme Telco, the OSS stack provides operational visibility into the physical and virtual network infrastructure.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

## P

**Payment Gateway**. A service that authorizes and processes payment transactions between a merchant and the payment networks (credit card networks, bank transfers, digital wallets). Acme Financial Services operates a payment gateway that serves both Acme Retail's eCommerce platform and external merchant clients.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**PCI-DSS** (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard). A set of security standards designed to ensure that companies accepting, processing, storing, or transmitting credit card information maintain a secure environment. PCI-DSS compliance is mandatory for Acme Financial Services' payment processing operations and Acme Retail's eCommerce checkout.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**Pick/Pack/Ship**. The three-stage warehouse process for fulfilling orders: picking items from storage locations, packing them for shipment, and shipping the package to the customer. Acme Distribution's warehouses use a combination of automated and manual pick/pack/ship processes, with optimization algorithms that minimize travel time and packing waste.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

**Policy Administration**. The system and processes used to manage insurance policies throughout their lifecycle — from quoting and binding to endorsement, renewal, and cancellation. Acme Insurance's policy administration system is the system of record for all active policies and is a primary focus of the modernization initiative.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**POS** (Point of Sale). The system used to process transactions at the point of purchase in a physical retail location. Modern POS systems integrate with inventory management, loyalty programs, and payment processing. Acme Retail's POS system operates across all physical store locations and feeds transaction data into the central analytics platform.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**Premium** (Insurance). The amount of money a policyholder pays to an insurer in exchange for insurance coverage. Premiums are calculated based on risk factors, policy terms, and actuarial models. Acme Insurance calculates premiums through its rating engine, which evaluates risk factors specific to each line of business.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**Product Catalogue**. A structured database of all products available for sale, including descriptions, pricing, images, and attributes. Acme Retail's product catalogue serves both the eCommerce platform and physical stores, and is the authoritative source for product information across all channels.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**Proof of Delivery** (POD). Documentation confirming that a shipment has been delivered to the intended recipient. POD may include a signature, photograph, GPS coordinates, or timestamp. Acme Distribution's delivery drivers capture proof of delivery electronically through mobile devices, and the data is integrated into the order tracking system.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

**P&C** (Property and Casualty). A category of insurance that covers damage to property and legal liability for injuries or damage caused to others. P&C is Acme Insurance's largest line of business, encompassing homeowners, auto, commercial property, and general liability products.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**Publishing Pipeline**. The automated workflow that processes content from its raw form through transformation, validation, and distribution to publishing endpoints. Acme Media's publishing pipeline handles articles, video, and multimedia content, applying metadata enrichment, format conversion, and distribution rules before content reaches consumers.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

## R

**Rating Engine**. A system that calculates the price (premium or rate) for a product or service based on defined rating factors and rules. In insurance, the rating engine determines premium based on risk factors. In telecommunications, the rating engine calculates charges based on usage records. Both Acme Insurance and Acme Telco operate rating engines tailored to their respective domains.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**Recommendation Engine**. A system that suggests products, content, or actions to users based on their behavior, preferences, and contextual data. Acme Retail's recommendation engine uses collaborative filtering and machine learning models to generate personalized product recommendations on the eCommerce platform and in marketing communications.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**Regulatory Reporting**. The process of preparing and submitting reports required by regulatory authorities. In financial services, regulatory reporting includes capital adequacy reports, transaction reports, and suspicious activity reports. Acme Financial Services maintains automated reporting pipelines to meet deadlines across multiple jurisdictions and regulatory frameworks.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**Reinsurance**. Insurance purchased by an insurance company to transfer a portion of its risk to another insurer. Reinsurance helps insurers manage exposure to large or catastrophic losses. Acme Insurance maintains reinsurance treaties with global reinsurers, and reinsurance data flows are standardized using ACORD formats.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

**Risk Engine**. A system that evaluates and quantifies risk based on defined models, rules, and data inputs. In financial services, risk engines assess credit risk, market risk, and operational risk. Acme Financial Services' risk engine operates in real-time for transaction screening and in batch mode for portfolio-level risk assessment.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**Route Optimization**. The use of algorithms to determine the most efficient routes for delivery vehicles, considering factors such as distance, traffic, delivery windows, vehicle capacity, and driver schedules. Acme Distribution's route optimization system recalculates routes dynamically as conditions change throughout the delivery day.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*

## S

**SEPA** (Single Euro Payments Area). A payment integration initiative of the European Union that standardizes euro-denominated bank transfers across member states. SEPA enables cross-border payments to be processed as efficiently as domestic payments. Acme Financial Services supports SEPA credit transfers and direct debits for its European banking customers.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**SIM Provisioning**. The process of activating a SIM card on a mobile network, configuring it with subscriber information, and enabling network access. SIM provisioning at Acme Telco is automated through the BSS stack and includes identity verification, plan assignment, and network registration.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**SKU** (Stock Keeping Unit). A unique identifier assigned to a distinct product for inventory management and tracking purposes. SKUs are used across Acme Retail for product catalogue management and across Acme Distribution for warehouse operations. A single product may have multiple SKUs if it comes in different sizes, colors, or configurations.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Retail*

**SNMP** (Simple Network Management Protocol). A protocol used for monitoring and managing network devices such as routers, switches, and servers. SNMP enables Acme Telco's network management systems to collect performance metrics, detect faults, and configure network elements remotely.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**SOX** (Sarbanes-Oxley Act). A United States federal law that establishes requirements for financial reporting, internal controls, and corporate governance for publicly traded companies. SOX compliance requires Acme Corporation and its subsidiaries to maintain auditable financial controls and accurate reporting. Acme Financial Services bears the most significant SOX compliance burden due to the nature of its operations.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**SSAI** (Server-Side Ad Insertion). A technology that stitches advertisements into video streams on the server before delivery to the viewer's device. SSAI provides a seamless viewing experience and is more resistant to ad-blocking software than client-side insertion. Acme Media uses SSAI for its ad-supported streaming content.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

**Subscriber**. An individual or business entity that has an active service agreement with a telecommunications provider. Subscriber management encompasses onboarding, plan management, billing, and service lifecycle. Acme Telco's subscriber base spans consumer prepaid, consumer postpaid, and enterprise segments.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Telco*

**SWIFT** (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunication). A global messaging network used by financial institutions to securely exchange information about financial transactions. SWIFT messages follow standardized formats (MT and MX) for payments, securities, and trade finance. Acme Financial Services uses SWIFT for international wire transfers and correspondent banking.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

## T

**Transcoding**. The process of converting a media file from one format or codec to another. Video transcoding is essential for delivering content across devices with different capabilities and network conditions. Acme Media operates a transcoding pipeline that generates multiple renditions (resolutions and bitrates) of each video asset for adaptive streaming.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

## U

**Underwriting**. The process by which an insurer evaluates the risk associated with a potential policyholder and determines whether to offer coverage, under what terms, and at what price. Underwriting at Acme Insurance ranges from automated rules-based decisioning for personal lines to complex manual assessment for large commercial risks.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Insurance*

## V

**VOD** (Video on Demand). A media distribution model that allows viewers to access video content at a time of their choosing, rather than at a scheduled broadcast time. Acme Media operates both subscription-based (SVOD) and ad-supported (AVOD) video-on-demand services, delivering content via adaptive streaming protocols.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Media*

## W

**Wealth Management**. Financial advisory and investment management services provided to high-net-worth individuals and institutional clients. Wealth management encompasses portfolio management, financial planning, estate planning, and tax advisory. Acme Financial Services' wealth management division serves clients through a combination of relationship managers and digital advisory tools.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**Wire Transfer**. An electronic method of transferring funds between bank accounts, typically used for high-value or time-sensitive transactions. Wire transfers can be domestic or international, with international transfers routed through the SWIFT network. Acme Financial Services processes wire transfers through its core banking platform with AML screening applied to every transaction.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Financial Services*

**WMS** (Warehouse Management System). A software system that manages warehouse operations including receiving, put-away, inventory management, picking, packing, and shipping. Acme Distribution's WMS coordinates activities across multiple warehouse facilities and integrates with the ERP system for inventory accounting and procurement.
*Primary subsidiary: Acme Distribution*
